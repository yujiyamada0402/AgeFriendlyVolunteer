Steps 5–6 · Training

Orientation training

After your pre-orientation education is confirmed, an Orientation Trainer will contact you to schedule your training sessions.

What to expect

Session 1 · Zoom

Zoom orientation with role-playing

An interactive online session covering the volunteer role, with role-playing exercises to practice real conversations.

Sessions 2–4 · In person

Approximately three in-person sessions

Hands-on orientation on the unit with your trainer, meeting patients together and gradually taking the lead.

What your trainer looks for

During orientation, trainers assess your readiness to volunteer independently, including:

  • Effective communication
  • Professionalism
  • Compassionate presence
  • Role-specific skills and behaviors
  • Readiness to volunteer independently

After your final session

Your trainer notifies the MD / Volunteer Lead that you are ready for placement, and you will receive your volunteer assignment by email.
